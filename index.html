Kathi Roll

Introduction Kolkata-style Kathi rolls are a popular street food originating from Kolkata, India, and have gained immense popularity across the country and beyond. The Kathi roll is essentially a type of Indian wrap or roll, consisting of various fillings wrapped in a paratha (Indian flatbread) or roti (whole wheat flatbread). Kolkata-style Kathi rolls are beloved for their delicious and flavorful combination of ingredients, making them a favorite street food not only in Kolkata but also throughout India and in Indian communities around the world.

Luchi

Introduction Luchi is a popular deep-fried bread that originates from the Indian subcontinent, particularly Bengal, Assam, Bihar, and Odisha. It is a staple food in Bengali cuisine and is often served as a breakfast item or as a snack during special occasions and festivals such as Durga Puja, weddings, and other special occasions. Overall, luchi is a beloved dish in Bengali cuisine, appreciated for its simplicity, versatility, and delicious taste.

Roshogolla

Introduction Roshogolla, also known as Rasgulla, is a popular and traditional sweet in the Indian subcontinent, particularly in the states of West Bengal and Odisha. It is a spongy and light dessert made from fresh chhena (Indian cottage cheese), which is kneaded and then shaped into small, round balls. These balls are then cooked in a sugar syrup until they become soft and spongy. Roshogolla is often served chilled and garnished with a sprinkle of cardamom or saffron for added flavor.
